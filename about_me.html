About Me

To quote Spider-Man: "Hey, everyone!"
I am currently a fourth-year Ph.D. student in the Astronomy Department at the University of Florida. I work under the supervision of Prof. Jason Dittmann on creating a single transit detection pipeline in Kepler. The final aim of my work is to find the frequency of Earth-like planets around Sun-like stars.

I was born and raised in Clearwater, Florida. I came to UF for my undergrad in the Fall 2018. I received a Bachelor's of Science summa cum laude in Physics with minors in Mathematics and Computer and Information Science and Engineering from UF in the Spring 2022. For my senior thesis, I studied the Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO) in the 2-point correlation function (2PCF) of galaxies. I came back to UF in the Fall of 2022 to pursue my PhD in Astronomy, this time in exoplanets.
